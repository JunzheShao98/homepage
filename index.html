About Me

I am currently a Ph.D. Candidate in Biostatistics at the University of California, Berkeley, starting September 2023. My research interests lie at the intersection of causal inference, time series analysis, high-dimensional data, and their applications in public health and medicine.

Previously, I received my M.S. in Biostatistics from Columbia University and my B.S. in Biological Science with a Minor in Physics from Peking University.

Right now, I am interested in causal inference including topics like semi-parametric theory, causal inference in more complicated data structures like interference and time-series, adaptive design and multi-arm bandit. I wish to incorporate statistics physics inspired methods for general causal identification and estimation problems, such as concepts from models like the Ising model shown below.
